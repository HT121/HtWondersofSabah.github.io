Most trips to Sabah start out halfway up the state’s western seaboard in the friendly capital, Kota Kinabalu. A grim and dense grid of concrete structures, KK’s citizens are dependably welcoming and its nightlife doesn’t leave much to be desired. When the sun goes down in Sabah, start out at the Waterfront Esplanade along Jalan Tun Fuad Stephens – besides fine dining, this upscale hotspot has a good number of ‘resto-bars’ including BED – the biggest dance club in town. Later head to Beach St. in the centre of town; chock-a-block with bars and eateries, this semi-pedestrian street has watering holes with pricey drinks, live local and Filipino bands and karaoke in the central round most nights.

1) Cocoon Restaurant & Bar

Just around the corner from the Hyatt Regency Hotel, Cocoon Restaurant & Bar is one of the city’s choice nightspots. This contemporary-themed bar is only a few years old and features a decent selection of slightly-overpriced drinks. Happy hour is between 17:00 – 21:00; this watering hole also serves food and there’s a live Filipino band. Afterwards the DJs play good R&B tunes till closing.

  • Opening Hours: 17:00 - 01:00 Sunday – Thursday; 17:00 - 02:00 Friday & Saturday

2) The Loft, Kota Kinabalu

Another bar along the pub-lined Waterfront Esplanade, this watering hole is a popular after-work and nightcap joint. Boasting incredible views of the sunset (especially if you snag a good table), the cocktail, beer and wine selection at this watering hole is extensive and there’s a pool table and big screen behind the bar which broadcasts European football matches. Happy hour is between 15:30 – 21:00.

  • Opening Hours: 15:00 - 01:00 Sunday – Thursday; 15:00 - 02:00 Friday and Saturday
  • Location: Waterfront Esplanade

3) Upperstar Pepper Grill & Bar

Just a few metres away from Shenanigans, this semi-outdoor artsy bistro dishes out simple local and western pub grub and cheap drinks. Besides the grilled cuisine, be sure to sample the pizzas from the Upperstar Pizza Corner – try the Upperstar Sensation. Featuring a young and yuppie clientele, there’s various memorabilia on the walls and the place is used to cater to big crowds; these days it’s a great place to enjoy good food along with a cold pint.

  • Location: Block C, Lot 8, Ground Floor, Lorong Okok Kayu Manis, Damai Plaza Phase 3, Kota Kinabalu
